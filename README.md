The Architecture of Unseen Knowledge
Integrating Invisible Research Theory and the Kallol Taxonomy for a Global Independent Research Ecosystem

A research initiative by Kallol Chakrabarti exploring how independent research can be documented, validated, organized, and made globally discoverable through open repositories, DOI registration, and reproducible knowledge frameworks.

Overview

This project presents The Architecture of Unseen Knowledge, a framework that combines:

Invisible Research Theory
The Kallol Taxonomy
Framework Science

Together, these concepts form a structured model for transforming undocumented or independently developed knowledge into a verifiable and globally accessible research ecosystem.

The project demonstrates how independent researchers can build visible, citable, and reproducible bodies of work outside traditional institutional structures.

Core Concepts
Invisible Research Theory

Invisible Research Theory examines intellectual contributions created outside conventional academic systems and proposes methods for making them:

Visible
Citable
Discoverable
Verifiable

through open-access repositories and persistent identifiers.

The Kallol Taxonomy

The Kallol Taxonomy is a classification framework designed to organize independent research outputs into structured and reproducible categories.

Its goals include:

Consistency
Knowledge organization
Discoverability
Long-term replication
Framework Science

Framework Science represents the integration of the two concepts above.

It focuses on building systems that transform fragmented knowledge into interconnected, verifiable, and scalable research ecosystems.

Research Objectives
Develop reproducible models for independent research.
Increase visibility of non-institutional scholarship.
Promote open-access knowledge dissemination.
Create structured pathways for knowledge verification.
Enable future researchers to replicate and expand documented frameworks.
