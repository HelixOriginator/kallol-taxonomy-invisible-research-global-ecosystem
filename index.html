The Theoretical Foundation

Two Pillars. One
Global Redefinition.

Kallol Chakrabarti's landmark preprint fuses two original constructs — Invisible Research Theory and the Kallol Taxonomy — into a single, verifiable architecture. The result is not merely an academic paper; it is a living benchmark that any independent researcher anywhere in the world can reproduce, verify, and build upon.

I
Pillar One

Invisible Research Theory

A theoretical framework that recognises the vast, undocumented intellectual labour generated outside formal academic institutions — and proposes mechanisms to make it visible, citable, and globally accessible through open repositories and DOI registration.

II
Pillar Two

The Kallol Taxonomy

A classification system for organising independent research outputs into reproducible, structured categories — enabling future researchers to adopt this taxonomy as a template, ensuring consistency, discoverability, and global replicability.

III
The Integration · Framework Science

Architecture of Unseen Knowledge

The synthesis of these two pillars creates what Chakrabarti terms Framework Science — a new discipline that transforms invisible, undocumented knowledge into a verifiable, globally navigable ecosystem. The preprint establishes the first reproducible model for independent research that achieves institutional-level rigour without institutional backing. It serves as the definitional benchmark against which all future independent research ecosystems may be measured.
